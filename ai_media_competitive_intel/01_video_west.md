## 3.3 Technology Deep Dive

- Flow-Matching Training: replaces standard denoising diffusion
- Reasoning Engine: Ray 3 can "reason" about concepts for complex prompt adherence

### Model Evolution

| Model | Release | Key Capabilities |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Dream Machine 1.0** | Jun 2024 | 1M users in 4 days |
| **Ray 3** | Early 2026 | SVT, 3D-VAE, flow-matching, HDR, reasoning |
| **Luma Agents** | Mar 2026 | End-to-end creative agents |
| **Dream Machine 2.0** | 2026 | Storyboard mode, camera control |

### Technical Differentiators
- First 16-bit HDR video generation
- 3D-VAE: native depth/occlusion understanding
- Storyboard mode for professional multi-shot workflow
- Camera controls: dolly, crane, orbit
- 3D heritage from 3D capture origins

### Infrastructure
- Project Halo: 2-gigawatt AI supercluster (Saudi Arabia, with HUMAIN/NVIDIA/AMD)

## Key Insights

1. **Two distinct markets:** Creative video generation (Runway, Pika, Luma) vs. Enterprise video communication (HeyGen, Synthesia)
2. **Capital efficiency matters:** HeyGen ($100M ARR on $70M funding) vs. Luma AI ($21M ARR on $1.07B) shows dramatically different unit economics
3. **Enterprise wins on revenue:** Synthesia and HeyGen generate the most revenue — clear enterprise ROI drives faster monetization
4. **World Models are the next frontier:** Runway and Luma both betting on world simulation as the long-term play
5. **Convergence ahead:** All companies expanding into each other's territory — avatar companies adding generation, generation companies adding avatars
