# Zootopia Creative DNA

## Story Structure
- **Act 1**: Underdog arrival + world reveal + inciting incident (recruitment)
- **Act 2A**: Partnership formation + investigation begins
- **Midpoint**: False victory (solved 14 cases)
- **Act 2B**: All is lost (conspiracy revealed, partnership breaks)
- **Act 3**: Reunion + climax (saving the city) + resolution

## Character Archetypes
- **Protagonist**: Judy Hopps (optimistic underdog, naive but determined)
- **Deuteragonist**: Nick Wilde (cynical trickster with hidden trauma)
- **Antagonist**: Bellwether (power-hungry bureaucrat, hidden until reveal)
- **Mentor**: Chief Bogo (gruff authority figure who becomes ally)

## Core Mechanism
Prejudice is a two-way street. Both predator AND prey harbor prejudice.
The con-man (fox) becomes the moral center. The prey (sheep) is the villain.

## Visual Style
- Vibrant, saturated colors (each district distinct)
- Soft ambient lighting
- Anthropomorphic but grounded animal design
- Scale differential (mouse town vs. giraffe district)

## Pacing Formula
- Setup-punch rhythm in dialogue
- Action sequences every 10 minutes
- Emotional beat at 25%, 50%, 75% marks
- Third act is 20% faster than first act

## Social Metaphor
Species = race/class. Predator/prey dynamics mirror real-world prejudice.
Message: "Change starts with me" — personal responsibility over systemic blame.

## Music Integration
- "Try Everything" as thematic anchor
- Score shifts with district (jungle drums, electronic synth, etc.)
- Silence used strategically in emotional beats

## Differentiation Seeds
1. Same structure, different setting (robots, aliens, etc.)
2. Same subject (prejudice), different angle (class, age, ability)
3. Same tone, different visual style (Ghibli 2D instead of CG)
4. Counter-take: Utopia IS real, but the villain wants to destroy it
