## 1. Qimen Power (奇门动力)

**Full Name**: Qimen Power (Beijing) Intelligent Technology Co., Ltd.  
**Chinese Name**: 奇门动力（北京）智能科技有限公司  
**Founded**: 2023  
**Location**: Chaoyang District, Beijing  
**Founders**: Zhou Yuyi (film industry veteran) + Fu Yu / "Wuheqilin" (digital artist with 10M+ social followers)

### What They Do

Qimen Power is the creator of **"First Prototype" (初号原型)** — the **world's first AI-native theatrical animated feature film**. Unlike companies that use AI as an assistant, Qimen Power builds every frame from script to final render using an end-to-end AI pipeline. They call this approach **"AI-native"** rather than "AI-assisted."

### Key Production: *First Prototype*

| Detail | Data |
|--------|------|
| **Runtime** | 85 minutes (full feature length) |
| **Team** | ~10 core members |
| **Production time** | 4 months |
| **Budget** | ¥2–3 million (~$300K–$420K USD) |
| **Traditional equivalent** | 200–500 people, 3–5 years, ¥20–30 million |
| **Efficiency gain** | **100x faster**, 60% cheaper, 50% shorter timeline |
| **Status** | Passed national censorship review (January 2026), expected theatrical release summer 2026 |

### Technology

- **Four AI "Black Boxes"**: Director AI, Art AI, Screenwriter AI, Cinematography AI
- **Virtual Personality System**: Proprietary character behavior engine
- **12 software patents** filed as of April 2026
- Combines large language models, diffusion models, real-time rendering, and emotional computing

## Industry Context: Beijing's AIGC Ecosystem

### AIGC Audiovisual Innovation Center (北京AIGC视听产业创新中心)

- **Location**: Langyuan Station (郎园站), Chaoyang District, Beijing
- **Opened**: August 2025
- **Purpose**: Beijing's first dedicated AIGC audiovisual industry hub
- **Services**: Computing power, AI agent pipelines, tech collaboration, talent training
- **Partners**: Beijing Film Academy, Communication University of China
- **Funding**: Chaoyang District has established a dedicated AIGC audiovisual development fund

### Key Industry Trends (2024–2026)

| Shift | From | To |
|-------|------|----|
| **Production model** | AI-assisted VFX | AI-native (script → final render) |
| **Team size** | 200–500 people | 10–20 people |
| **Timeline** | 3–5 years | 4–12 months |
| **Cost** | Tens of millions ¥ | Single-digit millions ¥ |
| **Efficiency** | Baseline | 100x improvement (Qimen Power case) |

### Legal Framework

- **Beijing Internet Court**: Has set legal precedent for copyright protection of AI-generated content
- **National Radio and Television Administration**: Implements tiered review system for AI-generated content
